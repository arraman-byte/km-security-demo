OAuth2 vs OpenID Connect

OAuth 2.0 is a delegated authorization framework: it lets an app obtain an access_token to call APIs on behalf of a user or itself. It says nothing about who the user is. OpenID Connect (OIDC) is a thin identity layer built on top of OAuth 2.0 that adds an id_token (a signed JWT describing the user), a userinfo endpoint, and a standardised discovery document.
